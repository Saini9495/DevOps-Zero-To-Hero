# Persistent Volume (PV) & Persistent Volume Claim (PVC)

## What is Storage in Kubernetes?

By default, Pods are ephemeral.

If a Pod is deleted, all data stored inside the container is lost.

To persist data, Kubernetes provides:

1. Persistent Volume (PV)
2. Persistent Volume Claim (PVC)

---

## Problem Without PV/PVC

Pod → Data Stored

Pod Deleted ❌

Data Deleted ❌

Example:

A MySQL pod stores customer records.

If the pod crashes, all records are lost.

---

## What is Persistent Volume (PV)?

A Persistent Volume is a storage resource in the cluster.

It is created by the administrator.

Examples:

- EBS Volume
- NFS
- Azure Disk
- HostPath

Think of PV as a Hard Disk.

---

## What is Persistent Volume Claim (PVC)?

PVC is a request for storage made by a Pod.

Think of PVC as a Storage Request Form.

---

## Workflow

Step 1:
Admin creates PV

Step 2:
Developer creates PVC

Step 3:
PVC binds to PV

Step 4:
Pod uses PVC

---

## Real Life Analogy

PV = Hotel Room

PVC = Room Booking

Pod = Customer

Customer never directly chooses a room.

Customer makes a booking request.

The hotel assigns a room.

---

## Access Modes

### ReadWriteOnce (RWO)

One node can read and write.

### ReadOnlyMany (ROX)

Many nodes can read.

### ReadWriteMany (RWX)

Many nodes can read and write.

---

## Reclaim Policies

### Retain

Data remains even after PVC deletion.

### Delete

Storage is deleted automatically.

### Recycle (Deprecated)

Volume cleaned and reused.

## Interview Questions

### What is PV?

A Persistent Volume is a storage resource in Kubernetes.

### What is PVC?

A Persistent Volume Claim is a request for storage.

### Difference Between PV and PVC?

PV = Actual Storage

PVC = Storage Request

### What happens if a Pod is deleted?

Data remains if stored in PV.

### Difference Between EmptyDir and PV?

EmptyDir:

- Temporary
- Deleted with Pod

PV:

- Persistent
- Survives Pod deletion

### What are Access Modes?

- RWO
- ROX
- RWX

### What is Reclaim Policy?

Defines what happens to storage after PVC deletion.
